Dorico Quick Reference

Write Mode – Part 2

Modify pitch or rhythmic position

Raise or lower pitch stepwise

  • Press Option-Up-Arrow or Option-Down-Arrow

The pitch is raised or lowered to the next scale tone, defined by the key signature, and respecting any prior accidentals in the bar within the same octave.

Raise or lower pitch by an octave

  • Press Cmd-Option-Up-Arrow or Cmd-Option-Down-Arrow

Change rhythmic position

To move the selected note(s) left or right by the increment defined in the current rhythmic grid:

  • Press Option-Left-Arrow or Option-Right-Arrow.

Modify duration of notes or rests

Normal input mode vs. insert mode

There are important differences in the behavior of changing note/rest durations depending on whether you are in insert mode or not.

  • When in normal note input mode, lengthening the duration of a note or rest can potentially erase (subsume) one or more notes or rests that follow.
  • When in insert mode, changing the duration of a note or rest does not affect notes or rests that follow.

Change the duration of a selected note or rest

  • Press the key command of the duration you want, or
  • select a different note value in the Notes panel.

To shorten or lengthen the duration of a selected note or rest by the increment defined in the current rhythmic grid:

  • Press Option-Shift-Left-Arrow or Option-Shift-Right-Arrow.

Double the duration length

  • Press Cmd-Option-Shift-Right-Arrow.

Halve the duration length

  • Press Cmd-Option-Shift-Left-Arrow.

Force duration

To force the exact note durations you input (instead of allowing Dorico to convert, for example, a quarter note on beat two tied to a half note to a dotted half note), select ‘Force Duration’ (the clamp icon) or press O to toggle it on, if necessary.

Add/delete bars, slurs & ties

Add/delete bars

  • Select the barline after which you want to add or delete bars.
  • Press Shift-B → popover: +1, -3

Add pickup bar

To create a pickup bar at the beginning of a flow:

  • Input the pickup note(s) plus at least one additional note or rest at the beginning of the flow.
  • Select the note or rest immediately following the pickup note(s).
  • Insert a barline before it by pressing Shift-B and entering | (the ‘vertical bar’ character) in the popover.

Slurs

  • Select the notes to which the slur applies.
  • Press S or select ‘Slur’ in the notes panel.

Tie notes

  • Select the note on which you want the tie to start.
  • Press T or select ‘Ties’ in the notes toolbox.

Untie notes

  • Select the note(s) you want to untie.
  • Press U or click on the ‘Scissors‘ icon in the notes toolbox.

Repeats, 1st and 2nd endings

Repeats popover

  • Select the bars that comprise the first ending.
  • Press Shift-R to bring up the 'Repeats' popover.
  • Type 'end' to create 1st and 2nd endings.

Working with voices

Show voice colors

Choose View > Note And Rest Colors > Voice Colors.

Start a new voice

  • Press Shift-V while in note input.
  • A small note symbol appears at the bottom left of the orange caret, showing a + sign and a downstem.
  • You can now start inputting notes/rests in that voice.

Select another voice

Once a voice exists, you can enter notes into it anywhere on the staff.

Within a particular bar, to cycle to the next voice, press V.

Once you have two or more voices active on a staff, you can cycle between inputting into any of them simply by hitting V.

Change the voice

You can change the voice of existing notes as follows:

  • Select the note(s) you want to move to a different voice.
  • Choose Edit > Voices > Change Voice > [Voice], or
  • choose the above options from the context menu.

If there is only one voice on the staff, you can create a new voice for your selected notes.
